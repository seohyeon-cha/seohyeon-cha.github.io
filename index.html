I'm a MS student in Electrical Engineering at KAIST, working as machine learning researcher at Advanced Radio Technology Lab (ARTLab) .


My research goals are oriented towards trustworthy and scalable machine learning to enable reliable real-world deployment of models. In particular, I am deeply interested in providing post-hoc explanations in decentralized scenarios characterized by data scarcity and heterogeneity. Additionally, I am keen on exploring the scalability of both datasets and models, tackling issues related to limited resources and unbalanced data in on-device learning. Through my research, I aspire to contribute to the ongoing advancement of safe and applicable machine learning practices. To view the list of papers I've contributed to, please visit the publication section.

Research interests
  • Trustworthy machine learning, with a focus on uncertainty quantification
  • Scaling down the models and datasets for on-device learning
  • Federated learning under system and statistical heterogeneity
